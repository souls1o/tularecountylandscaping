Patios fail when bases move—**expansive soils, poor compaction, and missing edge restraint** dominate warranty callbacks across the Central Valley. Heat cycles amplify movement: panels telegraph slight shifts into visible dips, trip hazards, and pooling that did not appear during mild spring installs.

High-intent homeowners should expect specs articulated in lifts, moisture conditioning, and compaction—not vague depth guesses. That documentation matters doubly when patios tie into [gravel landscaping](/services/gravel-landscaping) borders or irrigation trenches that cannot be relocated cheaply after the plate compactor leaves.

> **Key takeaway:** Structural base stone and decorative top dress are different jobs. Separate layers, compact in lifts, and plan drainage before pavers lock in.

---

## Excavate and build lifts to traffic—not vibes

Excavation depth varies with expected loads (dining sets and islands versus foot traffic only) and soil behavior. Your contractor should narrate lifts and compaction counts, especially near apron slopes where runoff concentrates during summer storms.

Photograph irrigation valve boxes and known utilities before cuts deepen; retrofits after compaction are expensive.

---

## Fabric roles, drainage plane, and irrigation honesty

Structural base stone locks under compaction; decorative chips belong above separation geotextile where specifications call for it. Mixing structural and decorative layers invites migration and hollow spots—particularly adjacent to gravel transitions where tires shuffle stone weekly.

Slope slightly away from structures or integrate channel drains where walls trap water. Pair patio scopes with candid mapping of existing [irrigation](/services/irrigation-repair) laterals so stakes and sleeves avoid chronic leak zones.

---

## Sleeves for lighting and outdoor kitchens

Plan conduit sleeves before final compaction when posts, kitchen islands, or step lighting will land on finished paving—retrofits through compacted base cost more than strategic stubs during prep.
